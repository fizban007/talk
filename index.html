Environments for Reconnection

We performed GRMHD simulations with Monte Carlo photons to investigate the magnetic and radiation environment around M87 (Yao, AC, et al 2021)

Environments for Reconnection

  • Magnetic field at the horizon $B_0 \sim 100\,\mathrm{G}$, $U_B \sim 400\,\mathrm{erg/cm^3}$.

  • Soft photon energy density near the horizon is quite high for MAD simulations, $U_\mathrm{rad} \sim 100\,\mathrm{erg/cm^3}$, even higher close to the equatorial plane. $\left<\epsilon_\mathrm{ph}\right>\sim 10^{-2}\,\mathrm{eV}$

  • $U_B \sim U_\mathrm{rad}$

Environments for Reconnection

  • Assuming a reconnecting region of $r_g^2$ with $v_\mathrm{rec}\sim 0.1c$, this produces a dissipation rate $\dot{E}\sim 10^{42}\,\mathrm{erg/s}$.

  • The highly magnetized funnel is filled with $e^\pm$ pairs. $\sigma_\mathrm{GJ}\sim 10^{13}$. Realistically pair drizzle can provide a multiplicity of $\mathcal{M}\sim 10^4$ (Wong et al 2021). Lets take $\sigma \sim 10^9$.

  • Radiation limited Lorentz factor $\gamma_\mathrm{rad} \sim 10^7 \ll \sigma$. This is reconnection in the strong cooling regime.

Radiative Reconnection

Goals: study strongly radiative reconnection in the presence of both synchrotron and external Compton cooling


  • What is the reconnection physics? How is particle acceleration affected by radiation?

  • What fraction of energy goes into synchrotron vs IC emission?

  • What is the resulting photon spectrum? Does it agree with what we see in M87?

Simulation Setup

2D double periodic box, Harris current sheet. Set upstream $U_B = U_\mathrm{rad}$ so that $\ell_B = \ell_\mathrm{IC}$.

All simulations resolve the upstream cyclotron frequency. Box resolution is 36864$\times$36864 with 20 ppc. Performed on OLCF Summit.

Synchrotron vs Inverse Compton Emissivity

Synchrotron vs Inverse Compton

In the limit $\gamma_\mathrm{rad} \ll \sigma$, current sheet thickness should scale with $\gamma_\mathrm{rad}\rho_0$ instead of $\sigma\rho_0$. Define $\ell_p = \sigma_T U_\mathrm{rad} (c/\omega_p) / m_ec^2$, then:

\[ \delta_\mathrm{cs} \sim 0.2\frac{c/\omega_p}{\sigma^{1/4}\ell_\mathrm{p}^{1/2}} \]

The cooling length of particles at $\gamma_\mathrm{rad}$ incidentally has the exact same scaling:

\[ l_\mathrm{cool} \sim \frac{3}{4}\frac{c/\omega_p}{\gamma_\mathrm{rad}\ell_\mathrm{p}} \sim 3\frac{c/\omega_p}{\sigma^{1/4}\ell_\mathrm{p}^{1/2}} \sim 15 \delta_\mathrm{cs} \]

Synchrotron vs Inverse Compton

Since cooling length is comparable to the size of elementary current sheets, energetic particles can't cool significantly before they enter the plasmoids.


We expect that even in the regime $\gamma_\mathrm{rad}\ll \sigma$, we still expect significant synchrotron emission.
